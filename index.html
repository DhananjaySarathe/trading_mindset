Phase 1: The Psychology of a Survivor

Reframe Losses as "Expenses"

In your corporate job, the company spends money to make money. In trading, losing trades are just your business expenses.

The Fix: Accept that a 50% win rate is highly profitable if winners outsize losers.

The Two Deadly Sins

  • FOMO: Buying at the top because you feel left behind.
    Rule: Missed the setup? Wait for the next train.
  • Revenge Trading: Doubling down immediately after a loss to "win it back".
    Rule: Two losses in a day? Shut the laptop.

The "Boredom" Indicator

Good trading is actually boring. You wait hours or days for a setup, click, and do nothing.

If your heart is racing and palms are sweating... you are gambling, not trading.

Phase 2: The Math of Survival

This is the most important section. If you follow these mathematical rules, it is mathematically near-impossible for you to go bankrupt.

The Asymmetry of Loss

Math works against you when you lose. Never let a small loss turn into a big loss.

If you lose 10% You need +11% to break even
If you lose 25% You need +33% to break even
If you lose 50% You need +100% to break even!

The Golden 1% Rule

Never risk more than 1% to 2% of your Total Capital on a single trade.

Capital: ₹100,000
1% Max Risk: ₹1,000
Setup: Buy at ₹100, Stop Loss at ₹95
Risk per share: ₹5
Math: ₹1,000 ÷ ₹5 = 200 shares
Result: If stopped out, you lose exactly ₹1,000 and live to trade tomorrow.
Aim for 1:2 Risk/Reward (R:R) If risking ₹1,000, target ₹2,000. This way, you can be wrong 50% of the time and still grow your account.

Phase 3: The 4 Fastest Ways to Go Bankrupt

Avoid these fatal errors at all costs. These are the exact reasons why 90% of retail traders lose their money.

1. No Hard Stop Loss

"It will bounce back, I'll just hold it." This turns day trades into forced long-term investments. Always set a system-level stop loss upon entry.

2. Averaging Down on a Loser

Throwing good money after bad. Only average up on winners. Never average down on day or swing trades hoping for a bounce.

3. Using High Leverage/Margin

Brokers offer 5x or 10x leverage. For beginners, this is financial suicide. It amplifies gains, but dramatically amplifies losses. Trade pure cash in Year 1.

4. Trading the P&L, Not the Chart

"I just need ₹2,000 more for a vacation." This forces trades that aren't there. The market doesn't care about your goals. Trade setups, ignore the money.

Phase 4: Your Action Plan

Treat this like a structured corporate project rollout.

1

The "Burn" Capital

Isolate your trading money. Put it in a separate account and mentally write it off. If losing it changes your lifestyle, it's too much.

2

Paper Trading (1-2 Mos)

Use platforms like TradingView with fake money. Prove you can execute your strategy without emotion before touching real cash.

3

Micro-Account (3-6 Mos)

Once profitable on paper, trade a tiny real amount (e.g., ₹10,000). Psychology changes instantly with real stakes. Learn to manage it.

4

Keep a Journal

A trader without a journal is a gambler. Record: Setup, Entry, Target, Stop Loss, Result, and most importantly: your emotions.
