Phase 1: The Psychology of a Survivor

Reframe Losses

In corporate, the company spends money to make money. In trading, losing trades are just your business expenses, not personal failures.

Paid for Patience

In a corporate job, you get paid for being busy and putting in time. In trading, you get paid strictly for waiting for the right setup.

The Deadly Sins

FOMO: Buying at the top. Missed it? Wait for the next train.

Revenge Trading: Doubling down to win it back. Two losses = shut laptop.

The "Boredom" Indicator

Good trading is boring. If your heart is racing and palms are sweating, you are gambling, not trading. Embrace the boredom.

Gambler Traits (The 90%)

  • Needs constant action & excitement
  • Reacts emotionally to every price tick
  • Trades randomly without a strict plan
  • Revenge trades immediately after a loss
  • Chases huge, unrealistic home-run wins

Trader Traits (The 10%)

  • Loves boredom and waiting for setups
  • Emotionally detached from the outcome
  • Strictly follows the pre-defined plan
  • Accepts losses quickly as a business cost
  • Focuses on slow, steady account growth

The 90% Rule (Mark Douglas)

"90% of trading is psychology, 10% is strategy. You can have the absolute best technical strategy in the world, but if your psychology is weak, you will lose money."

The Asymmetry of Loss

Math works against you when you lose. Never let a small loss turn into a big loss.

If you lose 10% You need +11% to break even
If you lose 25% You need +33% to break even
If you lose 50% You need +100% to break even!

Advanced Survival Rules

  • Max Drawdown Limit (5% per week) If you lose 5% of your total capital in a single week, you stop trading for the rest of the week. Period. Go back to paper trading to reset your mind.
  • Never Move Your Stop Loss Once a trade is entered, your Stop Loss is concrete. The only time you move a stop loss is to move it up to lock in profits, never backwards to "give it room".

Position Sizing: The Core

Never risk more than 1% to 2% of your Total Capital on a single trade.

Capital: ₹100,000
1% Max Risk: ₹1,000
Setup: Buy at ₹100, Stop Loss at ₹95
Risk per share: ₹5
Math: ₹1,000 ÷ ₹5 = 200 shares
Formula: Position Size = (Risk Amount) ÷ (Entry Price - Stop Loss Price)

Result: By buying exactly 200 shares, if you are stopped out at ₹95, you lose exactly ₹1,000 and live to trade tomorrow.
Aim for 1:2 Risk/Reward (R:R) If risking ₹1,000, your target must be at least ₹2,000. With a 1:2 R:R, you can be wrong 50% of the time and still grow your account.

Phase 3: The 4 Fastest Ways to Go Bankrupt

Avoid these fatal errors at all costs. These are the exact reasons why 90% of retail traders lose their money.

1. No Hard Stop Loss

"It will bounce back, I'll just hold it." This turns day trades into forced long-term investments. Always set a system-level stop loss upon entry.

2. Averaging Down on a Loser

Throwing good money after bad. Only average up on winners. Never average down on day or swing trades hoping for a bounce.

3. Using High Leverage/Margin

Brokers offer 5x or 10x leverage. For beginners, this is financial suicide. It amplifies gains, but dramatically amplifies losses. Trade pure cash in Year 1.

4. Trading the P&L, Not the Chart

"I just need ₹2,000 more for a vacation." This forces trades that aren't there. The market doesn't care about your goals. Trade setups, ignore the money.

Phase 4: Pre-Trade Execution

How to Analyze a Trade (Before Entering)

  • 1. The Trend is Your Friend

    Don't fight the overall market trend. If the market is bleeding, don't try to catch falling knives.

  • 2. Support & Resistance

    Identify key levels where price has historically reacted, bounced, or rejected.

  • 3. Price Action & Volume

    Are buyers or sellers in control? Is the volume supporting the current price movement?

  • 4. Confluence

    Don't rely on just one indicator. Look for 2-3 confirming factors aligning at the same time.

  • If it's not a "Hell Yes", it's a "No". Wait for A+ setups only.

The Pre-Trade Checklist

Ask yourself these exact questions before clicking the 'Buy' or 'Sell' button.

  • Is this setup part of my tested trading plan?
  • What is my exact, pre-determined entry point?
  • Where is my hard Stop Loss level?
  • Where is my Target Profit (at least 2x my risk)?
  • Am I strictly risking 1% to 2% of my capital?
  • Am I feeling emotional (angry, greedy, bored, desperate)?
  • Is there major economic news coming out soon that will cause unpredictable spikes?

No Leverage! Ever.

By buying 40 shares, you're only using 40% of your total capital. Leaving 60% in cash. This is how you survive. Never use leverage (margin) with a small account.

Your Strict Rules for the Next 90 Days

  • 1. Only Trade High Volume Stocks Nifty 50 or Next 50 only. Stay away from illiquid penny stocks.
  • 2. No Options! No Futures! Cash equity only. Options will destroy a ₹10,000 account in a week.
  • 3. Maximum 1 Trade Per Day Prevents revenge trading and overtrading. Win or lose, you are done for the day.
  • 4. Review Before You Buy Run through your pre-trade checklist. Every. Single. Time.
  • 5. No Averaging Down Ever. If you are wrong, take the ₹200 loss and move on.
  • 6. Walk Away Set the trade, put target and stop loss in the system, and close the app.
